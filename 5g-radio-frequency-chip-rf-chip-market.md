<p><p>The 5G Radio Frequency (RF) chip market is segmented into high-frequency and low-frequency types based on operational bandwidth. High-frequency RF chips operate in millimeter-wave bands, supporting faster data rates and higher capacity, making them ideal for ultra-dense urban environments and applications like autonomous vehicles. Conversely, low-frequency RF chips utilize sub-6 GHz bands, offering broader coverage and better penetration through obstacles, suitable for rural deployments and IoT applications. Both types are essential for achieving the full potential of 5G networks.</p></p>
